Understanding Color Vision Deficiencies

Deuteranopia (Deuteranomalous)

Insensitivity to green light due to missing or malfunctioning M-cones. It is the most common form of color blindness. Greens look beige, reds look brownish-yellow, and distinguishing between violet and blue is highly challenging.

Protanopia (Protanomalous)

Insensitivity to red light due to missing or malfunctioning L-cones. Red appears dark and brownish, orange/green can blend, and bright red might appear almost black or dark grey.

Tritanopia (Tritanomalous)

Insensitivity to blue light due to missing or malfunctioning S-cones. This blue-yellow deficiency is extremely rare. Blue looks green, and yellow looks pink or light violet.
